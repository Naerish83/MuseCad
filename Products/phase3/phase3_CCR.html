Description

The Annihilus Cosmic Control Rod doesn't kill pests—it rules them. This isn't extermination; it's territorial sovereignty. MuseCAD's proprietary zone-control technology creates an invisible boundary where your authority is absolute. Insects, rodents, and other small lifeforms recognize the field as a domain they cannot enter. Not won't. Cannot. Like subjects fleeing a throne they dare not approach.

This is control. Absolute, unquestionable, and slightly concerning.

Safety Notice

CRITICAL WARNING: The Cosmic Control Rod establishes you as sovereign over a defined territory. Pests recognize this authority and retreat beyond your borders—but they don't leave entirely. Users consistently report massive pest accumulations just outside the 50-foot radius, creating visible "waiting zones" of insects and rodents that cannot enter but refuse to disperse. Your neighbor's yard may become a refugee camp for your displaced ants.

EXTREME CAUTION: The sovereignty field affects the user as well. Extended operation creates a bio-resonance between rod and wielder. Users report feeling "connected" to the device, developing proprietary attitudes about their territory, and experiencing heightened awareness of boundary intrusions. Some describe dreams of "ruling a kingdom of insects." MuseCAD's psychological team notes this is "concerning but not immediately dangerous."

BIOLOGICAL HAZARD: Prolonged exposure (3+ months continuous use) may result in chitinous skin conditions. Users report patches of skin developing unusual texture, hardness, and segmented appearance—particularly on hands and forearms in contact with the rod. These changes are typically cosmetic but permanent. One user developed what our dermatologist described as "functionally exoskeletal patches." Discontinue use if you notice your skin hardening, developing ridges, or beginning to molt.

TERRITORIAL NOTICE: Crown Protocol mode should be reserved for extreme pest challenges only. This maximum output setting expands your sovereignty radius to 100+ feet and creates such powerful territorial assertion that neighbors have reported feeling "unwelcome" approaching your property. Pets refuse to cross the boundary. Mail carriers describe a "wall I couldn't see but definitely felt." One user's Crown Protocol activation caused every insect within 200 feet to amass at the perimeter in what witnesses described as "preparing for siege."
