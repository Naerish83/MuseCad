Description

CLEANLINESS WILL BE ACHIEVED. EXTERMINATE... STAINS. The DALL-E (Dedicated Automated Laundry Liberation Entity) represents MuseCAD's most aggressive approach to fabric care—a washer/dryer duo styled after the universe's most efficient exterminators. Each unit features a plunger-style agitator, threatening LED eye-stalk, and an attitude problem that treats dirt as an existential enemy.

The washer operates with ruthless efficiency, rotating through increasingly aggressive wash cycles while announcing its intentions in a mechanical monotone. The dryer completes the process with heat levels that feel almost vindictive. Both units communicate with each other in electronic screeches, coordinating their assault on wrinkles and stains with military precision. They will achieve cleanliness. Resistance is futile. Delicate cycle is for the weak.

Safety Notice

WARNING: The DALL-E units treat laundry as warfare. They will announce cycles with statements like "BEGINNING EXTERMINATION PROTOCOL" and "STAINS WILL BE ELIMINATED." This is intentional branding, not malfunction. However, the aggressive tone has caused pets to refuse entering the laundry room and children to believe the machines are "angry at dirt."

EXTREME CAUTION: Do NOT mix delicates with regular loads. The DALL-E washer interprets "delicate cycle" as a weakness and will attempt to convince you that "ALL FABRICS REQUIRE MAXIMUM AGITATION." Users who insist on delicate settings report the machine emitting disappointed electronic sounds and occasionally refusing to operate until "PROPER EXTERMINATION PARAMETERS" are selected.

COORDINATION NOTICE: The washer and dryer communicate during transfer. When you move wet clothes from washer to dryer, both units emit electronic screeches that sound disturbingly like conversation. Users report the exchange sounds "judgy," particularly if you've left wet clothes sitting too long. One user claims their units said "HUMAN INEFFICIENCY DETECTED" during transfer. We cannot confirm, but we don't doubt it.

CRITICAL OPERATIONAL WARNING: The units occasionally roll forward slightly during spin cycle, accompanied by the voice module announcing "ADVANCING TO ELIMINATE RESISTANCE." This is normal gyroscopic behavior, not actual mobility. The DALL-E units cannot climb stairs. Probably.
