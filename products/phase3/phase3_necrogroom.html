NecroGroom™ Pet Revival Kit

Reanimated Grooming System

Model Code: NG-PS-1983

⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ EXTREME Ontological & Ethical

[Editorial Note: HAL-9000 declined to review this product. V.I.K.I. approved with enthusiasm.]

Description

Sometimes dead is better. But sometimes your pet needs a trim. The NecroGroom addresses the eternal question: "What if grooming could transcend mortality?" Housed in a retro wooden "resurrection box" that doubles as both a pet grooming station and a philosophical nightmare, the NecroGroom uses... unconventional methods to ensure Fluffy looks their best. Forever.

The power light flickers ominously when your pet misbehaves—not as a malfunction, but as a feature. The device maintains a supernatural connection to pets who have been groomed within its chamber, leading to reports of "lingering presences" and nail clippings that seem to regenerate. MuseCAD's legal team would like to emphasize this is metaphorical. Probably.

Features

  • Resurrection Box Design — Antique wooden cabinet aesthetic with modern horror
  • Behavioral Synchronization — Power flicker correlates with pet misbehavior (location-independent)
  • Nail & Fur Regeneration — Clippings seem to... return. Don't think about it too hard.
  • "Sometimes Dead Is Better" Override — Emergency shutoff for ethical concerns
  • Spectral Grooming Mode — For pets who are "no longer with us" but still need maintenance

Technical Specifications

  • Chamber Dimensions: 24" x 18" x 20"
  • Power Consumption: Variable (150W-800W depending on... activity)
  • Flicker Frequency: Correlated with pet behavior (mechanism unknown)
  • Wood Construction: Aged pine with brass fittings
  • Grooming Tools: Self-sharpening (concerning)
  • Weight: 45 lbs
  • Interface: Vintage sliding latches, ominous humming
  • Range: Power flicker observed up to 3 miles from unit
  • Warranty: Void upon transcendence of natural law

Safety Notice

CRITICAL WARNING: The NecroGroom operates on principles MuseCAD does not fully understand and our engineers refuse to explain. Pets groomed within the resurrection box may exhibit unusual behavior including: staring at the box for extended periods, responding to commands they shouldn't be able to hear, and a persistent "wrongness" that you can't quite articulate.

EXTREME CAUTION: The power light flicker is not random. It knows when your pet misbehaves. Users report the flicker occurring when their pet is in another room, another house, or—in one case—at the veterinarian's office three towns away. We cannot explain this. Do not ask us to explain this.

EXISTENTIAL NOTICE: Some pets emerge from the NecroGroom subtly different. They look the same. They mostly act the same. But there's something in their eyes that wasn't there before. Something that remembers the dark. MuseCAD's animal behaviorist notes that this is "probably fine" and "they still love you, just... differently."
